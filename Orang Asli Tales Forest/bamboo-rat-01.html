Once Upon A Time

The animal kingdom was terribly divided by
endless quarrels between the big carnivorous
and small omnivorous mammals. At the root of
these squirrels was a seemingly unsolvable rights
and privileges. Which group were the true owners
of the forest? Who among them could rightly lay
claim to the land and all its bounty?

To the big mammals, whose members included
the tigers, leopards, and bears, the answer was
simple – theirs were the more powerful of the
two groups, and hence they were the rightful
owners of the forests.

The smaller sized mammals, such as the rats,
shrews, squirrels and civets, begged to differ.
Although lesser in size, they were certain that
their much greater numbers were cause enough
for them to assert ownership over the areas in
which they lived. Thus the quarrels dragged on
and on.

Until One Day

One day, fed up with the constant bickering, the
two groups resolved to settle the issue once and
for all. To facilitate this, they held a conference
where each group would be able to voice their
case in a fair and civil manner.

As I was a vegetarian and thus regarded a neutral
entity, he was elected to serve as the mediator.
The two groups were represented by the tiger
and the rat respectively.

To the big mammals, whose members included
the tigers, leopards, and bears, the answer was
simple – theirs were the more powerful of the
two groups, and hence they were the rightful
owners of the forests.
