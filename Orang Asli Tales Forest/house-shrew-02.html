Once Upon A Time

Being the “dwarfs” of the forest, the shrews
were easy pickings for the larger animals.
Mercilessly tormented by their foes, they
were constantly humiliated and even worse,
suffered high a number of casualties. Their
numbers dwindled steadily over time, and
soon enough, they found themselves facing
the very real prospect of extinction.

Time To Take Action

It will get worse if this continues, I have to do
something. First of all, I have to gather all the
other shrews.

I told them that we are facing a serious crisis,
and I worry that this may lead to the end of
this crisis. It’s obviously we cannot match the
enemy, because the enemy's strength and
scale surpass us, so if we want to survive, we
must change our way of life.

What a pity…
They were once plump and burly, now reduced
to but skin and bone. Their previously shiny
coats had lost their lustre, and many bore scars.
And I said, firstly, we must admit to the fact that
we cannot compete with the bigger animal for
food. Therefore, we have to amend our diet.
From now on, we shall cease to eat meat, but
consume only insects, worms and plants.

I told them, secondly, we must protect ourselves
from being preyed upon by the larger animals. To
achieve this, we will need to smell as distasteful
as possible so that they will think twice before
even coming near us. So from now on, after every
meal, we shall rub ourselves against the plant that
produces a very strong and horrible odour.

Last but not least, I told them the third thing,
we shall never sleep on the ground again, and
allow our tormentors to sneak up on us when
we are at our most vulnerable. Instead, we will
dig holes beneath the earth to sleep and to rest in.

Time To Take Action

However, I insisted. I listened attentively to their
doubts and grievances, and answered all of
their questions with careful deliberation.

Ignore Them

Finally, the shrews reluctantly agreed to at
least try out the plan. Gradually, the situation
improved, the food was sufficient for everyone,
and they became stronger, and they were also
well protected because of the smell.

And I, after this incident, I was now hailed as a
great leader whose foresight and wisdom had
ensured their survival.

End.
